Seizures

First aid for seizures involves ensuring the person's safety, protecting them from injury, and offering support until the seizure ends.

Here are the steps for providing first aid for seizures:

1. Stay Calm:
- Stay calm and reassure those around you. Most seizures are brief and will end on their own.

2. Ensure Safety:
- Clear the area around the person to prevent injury. Remove any sharp objects or obstacles that could cause harm.
- If the person is near a hazardous location (such as stairs or a busy road), try to gently guide them to a safer area, if it can be done safely.

3. Protect the Person's Head:
- Cushion their head with a soft object, such as a folded towel or clothing, to protect them from injury.
- Do not restrain the person or attempt to hold them down. Allow the seizure to run its course.

4. Do Not Insert Objects or Restrict Movements:
- Do not put anything in the person's mouth during a seizure. Contrary to popular belief, it is not necessary and may cause injury.
- Do not try to hold the person's movements or restrict their limbs. It is best to let the seizure progress naturally.

5. Time the Seizure:
- Note the time the seizure starts. If it lasts longer than five minutes or if multiple seizures occur without recovery in between, it is considered a medical emergency. Call for emergency medical assistance.
6. Observe and Reassure:
- Pay attention to the person's movements and the characteristics of the seizure. This information can be helpful for medical professionals.
-Stay with the person during the seizure and provide reassurance when they regain consciousness.

7. Provide Post-Seizure Support:
- Once the seizure ends, the person may be confused, disoriented, or fatigued. Speak calmly and offer support.
-If necessary, help the person into a comfortable position and ensure they have access to fresh air.
- If the person is injured, provide appropriate first aid and, if needed, seek medical attention.

8. Offer Emotional Support:
- Seizures can be distressing for the person experiencing them. Offer emotional support and reassure them that you are there to help.

9. Seek Medical Evaluation:
-Encourage the person to seek medical evaluation after a seizure, particularly if it is their first seizure, if the seizure was prolonged, or if there are concerns about their health.

Note: If you encounter someone having a prolonged seizure (status epilepticus) lasting longer than five minutes or if there are breathing difficulties or injuries, call emergency medical services immediately.

Remember, it is important to respect the privacy and dignity of the person having a seizure and maintain their confidentiality.

Note: This information is not a substitute for professional medical advice. For specific guidance on first aid for seizures and any other medical concerns, consult a healthcare professional or refer to trusted medical resources.
